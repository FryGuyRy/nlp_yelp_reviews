There are kebap places on practically every street, it seems, in Stuttgart.  While they all look good and typically are among the most inexpensive places to eat, I'm frequently put off by the small amount of meat that the typical kebap restaurant puts into your sandwich.

I'm pleased to say that this restaurant is the exception!  I got a "Yufka" (not sure what that means), and it was loaded with meat and vegetables.  In fact, it was so big, it was enough to feed both me and my wife.  Tasty, too!

This shop has the pre-requisites I like of a restaurant:  very clean; good service; friendly.  They have two main line of food service, donor, which is the middle eastern spice meat roll; and pizza.  I didn't try the pizza, but I have to say they sure did have a lot of different pizza choices.

I really liked the young counterman.  He was congenial, and smiled with me at my attempts to speak in German, and was in general quite helpful.笑
